Session 01  ·  Section 1

Jesus Reveals Your Value

Overview

There are many ways we come into a life of faith. Some encounter Jesus in a deeply experiential way through the witness of someone close to them or in a moment of personal searching. Others come through intellectual reflection, arriving at the conviction that Jesus is who he says he is. Others are formed within a faith tradition and encounter the living Christ through the liturgical and sacramental life of the Church.

Whichever path has led you to discipleship, it is of immense value to grow deeper in understanding what has actually taken place in our acceptance of Christ. Christianity has always been a radical way of being in the world, often one that stands in tension with the surrounding culture. This is because, at its foundation, Christ redefines who we are, or more precisely, reveals who we have always been from God's perspective.

Over the next eight sessions, we will explore what it means to be defined in Christ, to see God through the lens of Christ, and how these realities shape a way of life in the world. This journey is about unpacking the gift of salvation, what it means for us, how it forms our reality, and how it opens us to live in the freedom of the Holy Spirit.

Opening Questions
1 What led you to do this course?
2 What is one thing you are hoping to get out of doing this course, and how are you feeling about going on the journey?
3 In a couple of sentences, describe what identity means to you.
Teaching Summary

The Gospel is the story of God loving us and restoring us to our created value through union with Christ. God has intervened in our brokenness and drawn us back into relationship with Himself.

Key Themes
  • The Gospel means "good news" — God's announcement that freedom has come through Christ
  • We carry deep questions about identity: Who am I really? How do I know?
  • These arise because we've experienced conditional love and a fractured relationship with God
  • The Gospel speaks to this longing — God has intervened in our alienation to restore us to right relationship with Himself
  • We were created to receive our sense of worth from the Trinity, not from achievement or approval
  • Our fundamental need is to know we are loved and known
  • When unmet, we seek fulfilment elsewhere: in wealth, status, success — yet every substitute leaves us unsatisfied
  • We become captive to fear rather than grounded in love
  • God was willing to give up His own Son to redeem us — revealing our infinite value to Him
  • He sees us as His sons and daughters in our divine dignity
  • God is not angry with us; He doesn't hold our sins against us. He comes looking for us
  • Our value to Him has never been in question
  • Redemption means to be bought back or set free from sin, death, and the powers of evil
  • In Christ, false structures fall away and we're invited into the truth
  • God's love is unconditional and freely given — it is grace
  • We are no longer defined by sin or our past; we are sons and daughters of God

Discussion Questions

Discuss in groups of 3–4. Feel free to respond to all questions at once or take turns answering each.

1 How could this fresh understanding of God's love shape your sense of intrinsic value?
2 How can you respond in your everyday life to the idea that God loves you now as much as ever?
3 How can we pray with and for one another over the next 7 weeks?
Practice

Writing a Prayer Intention

This week your practice is to write a prayer in your journal that puts into words your heart's response to the goodness of the Gospel message. Try and sit with what arises when you think about what Jesus has revealed and write a prayer that responds to that. Include a clear intention for how you wish to grow, learn, and develop your relationship with Jesus over the next 8 sessions.

  1. Find a time and place where you can be alone with Jesus without distraction.
  2. Reflect on what you have learned in Session 1 and identify the specific concepts that particularly stand out to you.
  3. Put this into your own words by writing it down in your journal. Allow yourself to flesh it out without trying to formulate it too rigidly.
  4. Read back what you have written and try to notice the main themes and how they relate to you personally.
  5. Formulate a prayer that puts into language what your intention is for this course — including the way you desire to grow and develop your relationship with Jesus.

This exercise is not a test or something to be achieved. It is a way for you to put into language the hidden movements of grace already working in your heart. It should take anywhere from 5 to 30 minutes — take as much time as is possible and appropriate to you.
